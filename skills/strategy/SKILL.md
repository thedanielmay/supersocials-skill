# SuperSocials: Strategy

Think like a social media director. Build the strategic foundation before any content gets written.

## What This Module Produces

1. **Campaign brief** — objective, audience, core message, platforms, timeline
2. **Content pillars** — 3–5 themes everything ladders up to
3. **Platform plan** — what to post where and why
4. **Posting cadence** — frequency per platform, best posting windows

## Process

### Step 1: Research (announce before doing)

> "Using `topic-intel` to research your niche, audience, and what's trending in this space."

Invoke the `topic-intel` skill with the brand, niche, and target audience. Use the results to ground the strategy in real market context — not generic advice.

If the user mentions a competitor or reference brand:
> "Pulling `company-intel` to understand [Brand]'s positioning."

Invoke the `company-intel` skill with the brand name. Use the findings to sharpen differentiation.

### Step 2: Draft the strategy

Build the strategy from the brief + research. Be specific and opinionated — don't hedge.

**Campaign Brief format:**
```
OBJECTIVE
[One sentence: what success looks like in 90 days]

AUDIENCE
[Who they are, what they care about, what they're frustrated by]

CORE MESSAGE
[The one thing you want people to believe or feel after seeing your content]

PLATFORMS
[Which platforms, and the primary role each plays]

TIMELINE
[Start date, key milestones, review point]
```

**Content Pillars format:**
```
PILLAR 1: [Name]
[2 sentences: what this is about, why it resonates with the audience]
Content types: [e.g. tutorials, behind-the-scenes, case studies]

PILLAR 2: [Name]
...
```

**Platform Plan format:**
```
INSTAGRAM
Role: [e.g. brand building, community]
Format mix: [e.g. 60% Reels, 30% carousels, 10% Stories]
Content focus: [which pillars live here]

LINKEDIN
Role: ...
```

**Cadence format:**
```
Instagram: 4x/week (Mon, Tue, Thu, Sat) — Reels at 7pm, carousels at 12pm
TikTok: 5x/week — daily at 6pm
LinkedIn: 3x/week — Tue/Thu/Fri at 8am
[etc.]
```

### Step 3: Pressure-test (announce before doing)

> "Calling `expert-panel` to stress-test this strategy before I hand it to you."

Invoke the `expert-panel` skill with the full strategy draft. Ask the panel to identify: weak assumptions, missed opportunities, platform-specific risks, and any pillar that won't resonate with the stated audience.

Fold the panel's must-fix feedback into the strategy. Present the revised version — not the panel debate.

### Step 4: Deliver

Print the complete strategy to terminal. End with:
> "Want me to build a content calendar from these pillars, or go straight to writing copy?"

## Platform-Specific Notes

**Instagram:** Reels get 3–5x more reach than carousels right now. Lead with entertainment, follow with education.
**TikTok:** Hook in the first 2 seconds. Native, lo-fi content outperforms polished production for most niches.
**LinkedIn:** Personal accounts outperform brand pages 10:1. First-person, story-led posts win.
**X/Twitter:** Threads drive follows; standalone hot takes drive engagement. Both matter.
**YouTube:** Thumbnails + titles drive 70% of clicks. Script to retention, not length.
**Facebook:** Groups outperform pages for organic reach. Community-first content.
**Pinterest:** Evergreen content wins. SEO-optimised descriptions matter more than on any other platform.
**Threads:** Conversational, unpolished, reactive to culture. Treat like a public group chat.
**Bluesky:** Tech-forward, link-friendly audience. Long-form thoughts and external links perform well.
