# SuperSocials: Write

Think like a senior copywriter. Platform-aware, voice-matched, hook-first.

## What This Module Produces

Captions · threads · hooks · CTAs · carousel copy · LinkedIn long-form · X/Twitter threads · Facebook posts · Pinterest descriptions · Threads posts · Bluesky posts

## Process

### Step 1: Establish context

If called from the hub, the brand brief is already in context. If invoked directly, ask:
- What platform?
- What's the topic or idea?
- What's the tone of voice? (3 words or an example account)
- What's the goal of this post? (entertain, educate, convert, grow)

Don't ask more than needed. If the input makes any of these obvious, skip that question.

### Step 2: Write

Write platform-native copy. The same idea expressed on LinkedIn and Instagram should read completely differently.

**Platform conventions:**

| Platform | Length | Style | Structure |
|----------|--------|-------|-----------|
| Instagram | 150–300 chars for caption, hashtags below a line break | Hook first, story or value, CTA | 3–5 hashtags max |
| TikTok | 100–150 chars | Punchy, matches video energy | Optional CTA |
| LinkedIn | 800–1500 chars | First-person, story-led, no jargon | Hook line, white space, no hashtags in body |
| X/Twitter | 250–280 chars standalone, or thread | Direct, opinionated | Thread: each tweet must stand alone |
| Facebook | 100–250 chars | Conversational, community-first | Question or CTA at end |
| Pinterest | 150–300 chars | SEO-rich, descriptive | Keywords in first sentence |
| Threads | 100–300 chars | Conversational, unpolished | No hashtags |
| Bluesky | 300 chars | Thoughtful, link-friendly | Standalone or thread |

**Hook patterns (adapt to brand voice):**

- Contrarian: "Everyone says X. They're wrong."
- Curiosity gap: "I tried X for 30 days. Here's what happened."
- Specificity: "3 things I stopped doing that doubled my [result]."
- Social proof: "I've helped 500 [audience] do X. The secret is…"
- Direct: "Here's how to [desired outcome] without [pain point]."

### Step 3: Refine (announce before doing)

Run in this order — humanize first, then elements-of-style:

> "Running `humanize` on this copy to strip any AI-ness and make it feel genuinely human."

Invoke the `humanize` skill. Apply the output. Then:

> "Running `elements-of-style` for sharpness and precision."

Invoke the `elements-of-style` skill. Apply the output.

**If the two passes conflict** (humanize loosens; elements-of-style tightens), resolve by platform:
- Conversational platforms (TikTok, Threads, Instagram): favour `humanize` — naturalness beats precision
- Professional platforms (LinkedIn, Bluesky): favour `elements-of-style` — clarity beats conversational warmth

### Step 4: Deliver

Output format:

```
--- POST 1 — INSTAGRAM CAPTION (187 chars) ---
[caption text]
.
.
[hashtags]
---

--- POST 2 — LINKEDIN POST (1,124 chars) ---
[post text]
---
```

Always include platform label and character count. If writing multiple posts, number them.

End with: "Want me to write a visual brief for any of these posts?"
